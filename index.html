The reason we picked these dates

Gion Matsuri — Japan's biggest festival

Our five nights in Kyoto land right on the peak of a 1,100-year-old festival. Giant lantern-lit floats, thousands of people, street food everywhere, and a huge parade of 12-ton floats being hand-pulled through the city. This is the heart of the trip.

Good to know 心得

Trip survival kit

💴

Cash is king

Japan still runs on cash for street food, small restaurants, temples & the festival. We'll carry around $900–1,000 total, pulled in chunks from 7-Eleven ATMs.

🥵

It'll be HOT

Mid-July is 33–35°C and humid. The plan: early starts, AC breaks midday, cooling towels, fans, and lots of water. Wear light colors.

🎒

What to pack

  • Light, breathable clothes
  • Comfy shoes — 15–20k steps a day
  • Folding fan + cooling towel
  • Small day backpack
  • Portable charger
📱

Handy apps

  • Google Maps — trains & walking
  • Suica in Apple/Google Wallet
  • Google Translate (camera mode!)
  • Screenshot this page for offline
